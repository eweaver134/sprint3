Information on Robotic Technology


The field of robotics is an evolutionary form of disruptive technology that has been taking the world by storm ever since its beginnings in the 1950’s. Just as the complexity of these machines has far surpassed George Devol’s original model from 1954, so has society’s fascination with the world of robotics and its capabilities. Nowadays, robots are being used around the globe to perform tasks to aid humans in countless fields from something as simple as cleaning your house to the precise work of a surgeon. The field of robotics has entirely transformed the ways in which society functions and there is so much more evolution to come.


What Are Robots?

The exact definition of what makes a robot has been in contention for several years with many roboticists claiming different things. According to Miriam Webster’s Dictionary, a robot is defined as “a device that automatically performs complicated, often repetitive tasks (as in an industrial assembly line)”. There are certainly some robots that may fit this description, but others that don’t quite fit in this box. Though at one point, the field of robotics may have been able to be summed up in this manner, it has now outgrown such narrow proportions. Robots have the capability to do so much more than they used to, and their spread of skills is only growing.

The da Vinci system is a surgical robot that can be used to assist surgeons in performing minimally invasive surgery. The daVinci has three components: the surgeon console, patient cart, and vision cart. The surgeon console is where the surgeon is able to view the surgery area and control the instruments. The patient cart has the instruments used for surgery in addition to the camera. The vision cart is responsible for connecting the two other components as the communication hub and facilitates the high-definition 3D live video feed provided to the surgeon. With these elements the daVinci is able to function beyond normal human capabilities.


Conclusion:

There is no doubt that the future of robotics in our world is bright and fast approaching. There are so many uses for robots already, much more than the few examples we’ve included here, and there are infinitely more in development or will soon be thought into a reality. The world is changing, and so has the field of robotics. Robotic technology has come a long way from its adolescent beginnings in the 1950’s and has evolved with our world impeccably. Similarly, worlds of disruptive technology have begun to collide as more and more robots are being infused with AI. AI, which stands for artificial intelligence, is another form of disruptive technology that has been taking the world by storm. But, its collaboration with the world of robotics means transformative things for our world. By combining robotics and AI, the capabilities of robots have grown exponentially. It is due to AI that so many robots are able to function autonomously. Nowadays, robotic technology and artificial intelligence are nearly inseparable from one another, with their synthesis only making them more powerful and more useful. These two are truly the future of our world, a future that is already starting to take shape.



Sources: iRobot, Starship Robots, Intuitive, Boston Dynamics.
