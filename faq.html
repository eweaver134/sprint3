FAQs:


  • chevron_rightWhat is a robot?
    Robots are autonomous creations made of metal and wiring that do things based on the code input to them, they come in many different shapes and sizes, some looking more humanoid than others.

  • chevron_rightWhat are some useful things robots can do?
    Robots can be used to automate repetitive tasks and jobs often done by humans, additionally they can be used to deliver various types of goods, or do other various services.

  • chevron_rightHow expensive are robots?
    Some can be within the range of prices form $25,000 - $100,000 for industrial grade robots, however the exact price varies heavily depending on the type, function, materials, etc.

  • chevron_rightAre robots dangerous to society and jobs?
    Overall most robots are not dangerous to society at large, some people may lose their jobs to robots, however they also create new jobs for technicians and other mechanics who work on the robots.

  • chevron_rightHow conveniently can robots deliver things?
    They can do so in a semi convenient fashion using pathing algorithms and satellite data in order to get your goods delivered within a timely fashion, however the technology is still developing and thus has errors occasionally.

  • chevron_rightAre robots moral?
    Robots are made with a specific set of guidelines in order to prevent the misuse of their strengths, this includes including the rough guidelines of a moral structure in their code that, are based around the “Three Laws of Robotics” (A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm. A robot must obey orders given to it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law. A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.)

  • chevron_rightWill robots one day take over the world?
    Hopefully and probably not, unless you mean that they are used in people's daily lives and are omnipresent in society.
